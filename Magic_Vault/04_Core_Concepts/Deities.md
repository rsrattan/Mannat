# Deities

Supernatural beings considered divine or sacred, which are central to most religions and many magical traditions. Deities are often believed to possess extraordinary powers, knowledge, and influence over the natural world and human affairs.

## Roles and Functions
- **Creation:** Many deities are associated with the creation of the universe, humanity, or specific aspects of the natural world.
- **Governance:** Deities often preside over specific domains (e.g., sky, sea, underworld) and maintain cosmic order.
- **Archetypes:** Deities can embody universal archetypes, representing fundamental human experiences, emotions, and aspects of the psyche.
- **Moral Guidance:** Many religions attribute moral laws and ethical principles to divine command.

## Types of Deities
- **Gods and Goddesses:** Anthropomorphic or zoomorphic beings with distinct personalities, powers, and mythologies.
- **Nature Spirits:** Deities or spirits associated with specific natural phenomena, locations, or elements (e.g., nymphs, dryads, elementals).
- **Ancestral Spirits:** Deified ancestors who are revered and believed to influence the living.
- **Demons and Adversaries:** While often seen as malevolent, these entities also play significant roles in many pantheons and cosmologies.

## Interaction with Deities
- **Worship and Prayer:** Acts of reverence, devotion, and communication with deities.
- **Invocation and Evocation:** Calling upon deities for assistance, guidance, or to manifest their presence.
- **Offerings and Sacrifices:** Ritualistic gifts given to deities to show respect, seek favor, or appease them.
- **Rituals and Ceremonies:** Structured practices designed to honor, connect with, or influence deities.
