# Ceremonial Magic

Ceremonial magic, also known as high magic, refers to a broad range of magical rituals involving complex and lengthy preparations, often with specific tools, robes, and invocations. It is typically distinguished from folk magic by its formal, structured, and often hierarchical nature.

## Characteristics
- **Formal Rituals:** Involves precise sequences of actions, words, and gestures.
- **Symbolic Tools:** Utilizes consecrated wands, swords, cups, pentacles, and other implements.
- **Divine Invocation:** Often involves calling upon divine beings, angels, or spirits for assistance or knowledge.
- **Personal Transformation:** Aims not only for external results but also for the spiritual development and enlightenment of the practitioner.

## Key Traditions
- **Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn:** A highly influential magical order of the late 19th and early 20th centuries, known for its elaborate rituals and system of magical training.
- **Enochian Magic:** A system of ceremonial magic based on the 16th-century writings of John Dee and Edward Kelley, involving communication with angels.
- **Goetia:** A system of magic focused on the evocation of demons, often for practical purposes.
